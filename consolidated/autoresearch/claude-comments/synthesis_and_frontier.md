# Head complexity $H^{*}(f)$: synthesis and the sharpened frontier

*AI-authored capstone (under `claude-comments/`; modifies no proof file). Crystallizes the verified theory and states the open problems as precise, attackable conjectures with the best current attack on each. Companion to `h_star_knowledge_map.md` (full map) and `RESEARCH_LOG.md` (per-result log).*

## 1. The invariant and the sandwich (settled)

$H^{*}(f) = \mathrm{MFdeg}_{\pm}(f)$ (L16): the least $H$ with $f$ sign-represented by a tangent vector $P = \theta\prod_h D_h + \sum_h N_h\prod_{g\neq h}D_g$ to the variety of products of $H$ affine forms, with **admissible** factors ($D_h>0$ on the cube, one-sided slopes — the model's monotone bias, L13). The placement among classical invariants:
$$
\deg_{\pm}(f) \;\le\; \mathrm{tChow}_{\pm}(f) \;\le\; H^{*}(f) \;\le\; M_{+}(f)-1,
$$
where $\mathrm{tChow}_{\pm}$ drops admissibility (arbitrary affine factors).

## 2. Three things that are now theorems

**(A) $H^{*}$ is genuinely finer than threshold degree, explicitly and near-linearly.** Set intersection $\mathrm{INT}_n$ ($\deg_{\pm}=2$) needs $H^{*}=\Omega(n/\log n)=\widetilde\Theta(n)$ heads (L33-L35) and admits $H^{*}\le n-1$ (L45), so $n/(8\log_2 n)\le H^{*}(\mathrm{INT}_n)\le n-1$; the indexing function $\mathrm{IDX}_k$ ($\deg_{\pm}=O(\log N)$) needs $\Omega(N/\log N)$ (L43). Both lower bounds via the **shatter-rectangle** certificate (L37): a coordinate partition on which $f$ computes the membership predicate $[j\in S]$ forces $H^{*}=\Omega(s/\log s)$ by a localized Warren count. Positivity-free.

**(B) Two nonsymmetric families are pinned.** Disjoint monotone DNFs have $H^{*}=\widetilde\Theta(\text{terms})$ (L36, pairing L14 with L37); weighted bands $\mathbf 1[\theta_1\le w\!\cdot\!x\le\theta_2]$ have $H^{*}=2$ (L44, pairing L25 with the checkerboard L3). With symmetric $f$ ($H^{*}=C(F)$, L12), exact products $\mathrm{sign}(AB)$ and $\mathrm{sign}(AB{+}g)$ ($H^{*}\le2$, L41/L42), and $H^{*}(\mathrm{INT}_3)=2$ (L39), the exact-value catalog now spans several nonsymmetric classes.

**(C) The product-sign part of positivity is free at order 2.** Order-2 tangent forms carry a $\mathrm{GL}_2$ gauge symmetry (L40): the sign of $\prod_h D_h$ — the only positivity-specific obstruction surviving the free numerators — is always removable. So at order 2 the residual positivity question is purely about *individual* positivity + one-sidedness of the denominator pencil.

### 2.5 The organizing picture: what attention heads find easy vs hard

Two structural principles discovered this session explain the catalog and say *why* a function is cheap or expensive.

**The monotone corner detector (composition, L45).** A single admissible head, under the model's one-sided (monotone) bias, can sharply isolate exactly one *extreme* corner (all-ones or all-zeros) of a coordinate block: the atom $\psi=M/(\gamma+\rho\sum_{i\in A}\alpha^{w_i})$, $\alpha\in(0,1)$, is $\ge0$, spikes on the all-ones corner and is $\approx0$ off it. Consequences: (i) ORing a disjoint *monotone* term costs $\le1$ head, so $H^{*}(f\vee g)\le H^{*}(f)+s$ for a disjoint monotone DNF of $s$ terms — giving $H^{*}(\mathrm{INT}_n)\le n-1$; (ii) but a *mixed-polarity* term (a parity, or an addressing/equality match) is **not** corner-detectable by one head, which is the structural reason $\mathrm{IDX}_k$ and parities are expensive. A single term in isolation is always an LTF ($H^{*}=1$); the polarity cost is a *composition* phenomenon.

**Linearization, and what is actually hard (the easy/hard dichotomy for pair-predicates).** On $2n$ bits $(x,y)$, a predicate is cheap iff it collapses to a single linear comparison of the two strings. With super-increasing weights the integer $I(x)$ has distinct subset sums, so any function of the difference $d=I(x)-I(y)$ has $H^{*}\le C(G)$ **and bounded sign-rank** (L47): equality $\mathrm{EQ}_n=\mathbf 1[d=0]$ has $H^{*}=2$ (L46), $\mathrm{GT}=\mathbf 1[d>0]$ has $H^{*}=1$, bands $\le2$; even **integer addition** is cheap — every output bit of $x+y$ has $H^{*}\le3$ (L48), the carry being itself a threshold of a positive weighted sum. The provably **hard** predicates ($\widetilde\Theta(n)$) are exactly the ones whose OR/addressing structure embeds the **membership predicate** $[j\in S]$ — a *shatter-rectangle* (L37): intersection $\mathrm{INT}_n=\bigvee_i(x_i\wedge y_i)$ (so $A_{+}=2^n-1$) and subset $x\subseteq y=\neg\mathrm{INT}_n(x,\bar y)$. *Mere bilinearity is not enough:* a lone bilinear coincidence such as interior Hamming $\mathbf 1[\mathrm{Hamming}=1]=\mathbf 1[\langle\chi,\xi\rangle=n-2]$ has $\deg_\pm\le4$ but only **linear** sign-rank ($\le n+2$, rigorous) and admits no shatter-rectangle (Hamming's global distance-count blocks the $[j\in S]$ gadget), so it gives at most an $\Omega(\log n)$ separation — and whether even that holds is open (its sign-rank may be bounded; all spectral tools are provably $O(1)$). *The one-line summary of $H^{*}$:* attention heads cheaply compare two encoded integers (a linear functional of each string), and pay $\widetilde\Theta(n)$ only for genuine **membership/addressing** structure — not for an arbitrary degree-2 coincidence.

**Arithmetic and order statistics under $H^{*}$ (a coherent sub-picture, this session).** The basic arithmetic/relational primitives on integers are all **constant-head**, with reliable threshold degree $\deg_{\pm}$ as the diagnostic (the heuristic search over-reports and must not be trusted): comparison $\mathrm{GT}$ ($H^{*}\le1$, L47), addition ($\le3$, L48) and subtraction (same, $\deg_{\pm}\le3$) — the carry chain is free since the carry is a threshold of a positive weighted sum — and the small **order statistics**: $\min,\max$ ($H^{*}=2$, L49, a comparison-gated selection of a single bit via $\mathrm{sign}(AB+g)$, $A$ one-sided) and the median of three ($H^{*}=3$: $\deg_{\pm}=3$ stays constant across $2,3,4$ bits-per-integer and the search confirms $=3$; "$a$ is the median" $=\mathbf 1[(a-b)(a-c)\le0]$ is a single product-sign, L41). *But "order statistics are cheap" does **not** extend unboundedly:* for the median of **four** integers $\deg_{\pm}$ already grows with the bits-per-integer ($3\to4$ from $2\to3$ bits), so the $k$-th-of-$m$ statistic is plausibly *not* constant-head for $m\ge4$ — rank ($=$ an exact count of comparison-LTFs) is the same kind of LTF-combination that makes AND-of-two-LTFs hard. So only the smallest order statistics ($m\le3$) are established cheap; the general case is open and likely grows. The sharp contrast is **multiplication** and **inner product**, which are *not* functions of $O(1)$ comparisons at all: their middle/output bits have $\deg_{\pm}=\Omega(n)$ (verified growing $1,3,4$ and $2,3,4$ for $n=2,3,4$), hence $H^{*}=\Omega(n)$. So among arithmetic operations the easy/hard line is **additive-and-comparison-based (cheap) vs genuinely multiplicative/bilinear (hard)** — the same line as the pair-predicate dichotomy.

## 3. The central open problem: does positivity cost? (F4)

**Conjecture F4-free.** $H^{*}(f) = \mathrm{tChow}_{\pm}(f)$ for all $f$ — i.e. $H^{*}$ equals the standard (positivity-free) tangential-Chow sign-rank. *This is the gateway to the final characterization:* if true, $H^{*}$ is a known-flavored algebraic invariant.

**State of evidence (strongly toward true):**
- Free at $H\le1$ (L30), symmetric (L31), wherever $\deg_{\pm}=H^{*}$ (L32).
- The $\sigma$-twist (the reduced obstruction) is *free at orders 2 and 3* by reliable targeted experiment (construct $f$ from a $\sigma$-twisted tChow form, find an admissible form — the reliable direction), including the hardest balanced twists at $n\le6$.
- Every $H^{*}$ lower bound we have (degree, flattening, counting, shatter-rectangle) is positivity-free, so "no gap found" is nearly vacuous — yet the *finite-cube absorption* mechanism ($x_i^2=x_i$) that produces freeness has **no algebraic/Zariski analogue**, explaining why the nonnegative-rank/border-rank gap intuition does *not* transfer.
- *Why the positivity-blindness is structural, not incidental (sharpened this session):* the only source of a **strong** ($\omega(1)$) $H^{*}$ lower bound we possess is the **membership/shatter-rectangle** structure (L37) — a coordinate partition embedding $[j\in S]$ — and that structure is realized **identically** by admissible and arbitrary-affine (tChow) forms (the Warren/sign-pattern count that drives L37 never references one-sidedness). The interior-Hamming study made this concrete: a function that is "hard-looking" (bilinear) but lacks membership structure has only *linear* sign-rank and yields no strong bound at all. So a positivity-aware lower bound would have to detect hardness from a source **other than** membership/addressing — and every such candidate source we know is either positivity-free or provably dead at order 2 (gauge invariance L40, free-numerator absorption). This is the precise reason F4 resists: our entire hardness vocabulary is positivity-blind.

**The precise residual (order 2).** $H^{*}(f)\le2 \iff \Pi(f)\cap\mathcal A\ne\emptyset$, where $\Pi(f)$ = representing 2-planes of affine forms (nonempty iff $\mathrm{tChow}_{\pm}(f)\le2$) and $\mathcal A$ = admissibly-spannable planes. Rigorously: $\mathcal A$ and $\Pi(f)$'s ambient variety are **equidimensional with identical tangent structure** (so no dimension/transversality argument decides it) and $\mathcal A$ is **not dense** (so no perturbation argument works). The conjecture is the **topological covering statement** that $\Pi(f)$ cannot be trapped in the non-admissible region — proved for $n\le4$, open for $n\ge5$, true in all reliable computation ($n\le6$). *A correct proof needs a genuinely new connectivity/covering tool, or (to disprove) the first positivity-aware lower bound.*

> Caveat banked this session: a "$\mathrm{tChow}_{\pm}\le r\Rightarrow H^{*}\le r+1$" shortcut is **false** — a sum of $K$ admissible-denominator products is an order-$K$ tangent form only for $K=2$ (the $H^{*}\le K$ cleared form is degree $K$).

**A concrete order-3 probe — the median bit (L50).** The cleanest finite-function test of F4 found this session. The median bit of three integers has a clean *symmetric cubic* tangent witness $P_j=s_x(B{-}C)^2+s_y(A{-}C)^2+s_z(A{-}B)^2$, so $\mathrm{tChow}_{\pm}=3$ — yet the witness's denominators are the three pairwise differences $A{-}B,A{-}C,B{-}C$, a **mixed-sign triangle that no input flip can make simultaneously one-sided** (the obstruction is the three-way coupling, intrinsic). Unlike the abstract order-2 covering statement, this is a *single explicit function* where positivity is visibly blocked at the natural witness. The numerics are tantalizingly split: heavy admissible-form search finds $H^{*}=3$ with healthy margin at $2$-bit integers ($n=6$, **F4-free**), but **fails** (margin $\approx0$, two parametrizations) at $3$-bit integers ($n=9$) — *either* F4-freeness via admissible denominators unrelated to the witness *or* the long-sought first positivity gap. (Heuristic infeasibility is not proof — the project has repeatedly seen search fail where a construction exists — so this is a sharply-posed open candidate, not a resolution.) **This is now the most promising concrete handle on F4:** resolve $H^{*}(\mathrm{MED}_j)$ — find the admissible order-3 form for all $n$ (F4-free, and a template for general admissible constructions), or prove its nonexistence (a positivity-aware lower bound, which would refute F4 and is exactly the missing tool §4.3 calls for).

## 4. The other open problems

1. **Rate of $H^{*}(\mathrm{INT}_n)$: $\Theta(n)$ vs $\Theta(n/\log n)$.** The **upper side is now rigorous**: $H^{*}(\mathrm{INT}_n)\le n-1$ (L45, disjoint-term composition inducted from $H^{*}(\mathrm{INT}_3)=2$), so $n/(8\log_2 n)\le H^{*}(\mathrm{INT}_n)\le n-1$, matching the exact value $n-1$ at every reliably computed $n$ ($n\le5$). The **only** remaining gap is the constant-vs-$\log$ factor in the *lower* bound: the shatter-rectangle / Warren method is **provably capped at $\Omega(n/\log n)$** (Bartlett-Maiorov-Meir bit-extraction makes the $\log$ necessary for the abstract sign-family), so closing to $\Theta(n)$ needs a lower bound using the *tangent-form rigidity* (shared $\theta$, tied $N_h/D_h$, one $P$ across all columns) that Warren ignores; closing to $\Theta(n/\log n)$ needs a tangent-form bit-packing construction. *The $n-1$ saving is provably irreducibly multiplicative:* the positive-alternation number $A_{+}(\mathrm{INT}_n)=2^n-1$, so no weighted-score (additive-pairing-blind) route gives any polynomial bound — consistent with the multiplicative degree-$(n-1)$ products L45 actually uses.
2. **Exact characterization of $H^{*}$ for general nonsymmetric $f$.** No alternation/sign-change invariant equals it ($A_{+}$ over-counts, §7 of the map; $\mathrm{sign}$ changes of a fixed weighted sum over-count, verified). The exact invariant is the tangential-Chow rank (L16); whether it reduces to $\mathrm{tChow}_{\pm}$ is exactly F4.

   **An attractive "affine-dimension" characterization — and why it is FALSE (a conjecture tested and refuted this session).** It is tempting to conjecture $H^{*}(f)=O(1)$ iff $f$ is a bounded-cell function of $O(1)$ affine forms $f=F(L_1,\dots,L_k)$, $k=O(1)$. For $k=1$ this is *exact* and proven — $H^{*}=$ the sign-change count of $F$ along the single score (symmetric $F(|x|)$, L12; any positive weighted sum, L25) — and it correctly places every easy banked function ($k=1$: equality, bands, integer comparison, addition bits, L44/L46/L47/L48; $k=2$: products $\mathrm{sign}(AB)$, L41) and every hard one ($\mathrm{INT}_n$, $\mathrm{IDX}_k$, disjoint DNF need $\omega(1)$ forms). **But the $\Leftarrow$ direction is false for $k\ge2$.** Since the cleared $H$-head form is a degree-$H$ PTF, $H^{*}(f)\ge\deg_{\pm}(f)$; and Sherstov ([arXiv:0910.4224](https://arxiv.org/abs/0910.4224)) constructs two halfspaces whose intersection has threshold degree $\Omega(n)$ — so that AND of two LTFs, a **one-cell function of just two affine forms**, has $H^{*}=\Omega(n)$, unbounded. *(Cautionary: small-$n$ numerics are misleading here — random and majority-based AND-of-2-LTFs stay at $\deg_{\pm}=2$, $H^{*}\le2$ through $n\le12$; the hard instances are special constructions whose growth $\Omega(n)$ is asymptotic. This is the same small-$n$ over-confidence trap that the equality/Hamming controls flagged; a Codex consult supplied the Sherstov counterexample.)* **Conclusion:** low affine dimension is *necessary but not sufficient* for low $H^{*}$ — the easy/hard dichotomy (weighted-comparison vs membership) is a faithful *intuition*, but the only exact invariant remains the tangential-Chow rank (L16). A clean corollary: $H^{*}(f\wedge g)\le H^{*}(f)+H^{*}(g)$ **fails even for disjoint LTFs** (an intersection of two halfspaces placed on disjoint blocks has $H^{*}=\Omega(n)$ while each LTF has $H^{*}=1$) — disjoint composition is subadditive only for *terms/clauses* (L45), not general functions.
3. **A positivity-aware lower bound** — the single missing tool. Every candidate mechanism (boundedness, monotone certificates, border/nonnegative-rank, Positivstellensatz) is provably dead at order 2 (gauge invariance, free-numerator absorption, openness of finite-cube sign-rep, affine-is-own-certificate). Its existence would resolve F4 (if a gap exists); its provable nonexistence at every order would essentially prove F4-free.

## 5. The most promising next moves
- **For F4 — the median bit (L50) is the sharpest concrete handle.** Resolve $H^{*}(\mathrm{MED}_j)$: either construct the admissible order-3 form for all $n$ (F4-free, and a reusable template for building admissible forms when the natural tangent denominators are inadmissible), or rigorously prove no such form exists for $n=9$ (the first positivity gap, refuting F4). A *single explicit function* with a clean tChow witness and visibly-blocked positivity — far more tractable to attack than the abstract order-2 covering statement. The decisive sub-step is a reliable (non-heuristic) feasibility verdict for the admissible 3-form at $n=9$.
- **For F4-free (general):** develop the connectivity/covering tool for $\Pi(f)$ vs $\mathcal A$ at order 2 (a semialgebraic-topology statement), then lift across orders.
- **For the $\mathrm{INT}_n$ rate:** the upper side is closed ($\le n-1$, L45); only a tangent-rigidity *lower* bound (the open $\Omega(n)$) — or an $O(n/\log n)$ tangent-form bit-packing construction — remains to pin the rate.
- **For breadth:** the shatter-rectangle applies to any "membership/addressing" structure; new explicit lower bounds are cheap there. Exact values come from pairing the upper toolkit (L9/L14/L19/L25) with the lower (L3/L6/L37) where they meet.
