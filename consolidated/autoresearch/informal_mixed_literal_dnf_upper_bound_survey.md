## Literature survey: Mixed-Literal DNF Subcube Upper Bound ($H^{*}(\bigvee_r \chi_{P_r,N_r}) \le s$)

### Verdict up front
This target is **not yet proved** in the stack, and — importantly — it is the **one upper bound the lead (Codex) has deliberately deferred at every iteration since Lemma 17.** The research log calls it "the genuinely hard mixed-literal subcube gadget" and pins the reason precisely (Iter 5): *"admissible denominators cannot count violations of both signs in one head."* So this is the open frontier, not redone work. The closest proved results are:

- **Lemma 36** (`036_…`): a *single* arbitrary mixed-literal subcube $\chi_{P,N}$ is **exactly** one head — but via the LTF characterization (Lemma 11), *not* via a bump. Iter 22's note is the key framing: "the remaining mixed-literal DNF obstacle is **calibration and aggregation** of subcube indicators, not recognition of a single mixed subcube."
- **Lemma 29 / 18 / 17**: $H^{*}\le s$ for DNFs / signed thresholds of subcubes where **each term is single-polarity** ($P_r=\emptyset$ or $N_r=\emptyset$). Lemma 29 ends with the explicit disclaimer: "This does not cover general subcubes with mixed literal polarities inside a single term."

So the target = exactly the gap between Lemma 36 ($s=1$, mixed) and Lemma 29 (any $s$, single-polarity per term).

### Why the proved technique provably does not extend (the core obstruction)
Lemmas 17/18/29 all use the same gadget: realize each term by a **reciprocal bump** $\phi_r = c_r/D_r$, $D_r=1+\sum_i\beta_{r,i}\nu_{r,i}$, with each $\phi_r\approx c_r$ on subcube $r$ and $\approx 0$ off it (an $\ell_\infty$ soft-indicator), then sum and threshold. This fails for mixed terms for a structural, model-level reason worth stating crisply, because it tells the lead which avenues are dead:

1. **A single head has sign-uniform bit-slope.** From `model.md`, the key logit is $\ell^{(h)}_i = a^{(h)} x_i + b^{(h)}_i$ where $a^{(h)}=(q^{(h)})^\top W_K^{(h)}(e_1-e_0)$ is **independent of position $i$**; position embeddings only move the bias $b_i$. Hence $\exp(\ell_i)=\rho_i\,\alpha^{x_i}$ with shared $\alpha=e^{a}>0$. This is exactly Lemma 13's "admissible denominator must be sign-uniform" and is why one head can deepen its softmax toward only **one** full-cube corner direction. A mixed subcube is anchored at an *interior* corner, unreachable as a denominator minimum.
2. **The numerator obeys the antipode/parallelogram law.** The atom's numerator is affine, so on any 2-coordinate restriction it satisfies Lemma 2: $N(00)+N(11)=N(01)+N(10)$. Therefore if a single atom is $\approx 0$ at three corners of a 2-subcube, it is forced $\approx 0$ at the fourth. Consequently **no single admissible atom can $\ell_\infty$-approximate a mixed bump** (e.g. $x_1(1-x_2)$): you cannot be near-1 on the mixed corner and near-0 on the other three. This is the quantitative form of the checkerboard obstruction (Lemma 3), which is itself the classical **Minsky–Papert** fact that one linear threshold cannot localize an interior corner / compute XOR (*Perceptrons*, 1969).

The same sign-uniformity also kills the obvious fallbacks: the mixed violation count $V_r=\sum_{N_r}x_j-\sum_{P_r}x_i+|P_r|$ is a *mixed-sign* affine function, which is **not** a single atom (constant-denominator atoms force sign-uniform numerator coefficients — Lemma 13). Splitting each term into a positive-violation detector and a negative-violation detector costs **2 heads per mixed term** ($\le 2s$), and even that does **not** aggregate: $\bigvee_r\chi_r=\mathbf 1[\sum_r\chi_r\ge1]$ with $\chi_r=\mathbf 1[b^+_r+b^-_r\ge \tfrac32]$ is a *max-of-sums*, not a single linear threshold of the $2s$ bumps (a quick two-term example breaks any global linear threshold). So the AND-within-term genuinely resists linear aggregation.

### Where the target sits in standard mathematics
- **It is OR-subadditivity for subcubes.** Since each $\chi_{P_r,N_r}$ has $H^{*}=1$ (Lemma 36), the target is *literally* the statement $H^{*}(\bigvee_r\chi_r)\le\sum_r H^{*}(\chi_r)$. The natural generalization is **$H^{*}(f\vee g)\le H^{*}(f)+H^{*}(g)$**, which would imply (with complement-invariance, Lemma 31, giving the AND/CNF dual) the clean bound $H^{*}(f)\le \min(\mathrm{DNFsize}(f),\mathrm{CNFsize}(f))$. This is *consistent* with all known lower bounds (random $f$: $H^{*}\sim 2^n/n^2$ by Lemma 24 vs DNF-size $\sim 2^n/n$; parity: $H^{*}=n$ vs $2^{n-1}$ terms), so it is plausibly true — but it is **not proved and not obvious** (OR is not subadditive for general threshold representations). I'd treat general OR-subadditivity as a target in its own right; proving it settles this immediately, and a counterexample would be very informative.
- **Boolean-function background**: a DNF term = monomial = subcube indicator; $f^{-1}(1)$ is a **subcube cover** of size $s$. Standard references: O'Donnell, *Analysis of Boolean Functions* (2014); Jukna, *Boolean Function Complexity* (2012). The "first matching term" reformulation connects to **decision lists** (Rivest, 1987) and suggests a telescoping construction.
- **Threshold-circuit analogue**: $f=\mathbf 1[\sum_r\chi_r\ge 1]$ is a depth-2 **THR∘AND** circuit with $s$ bottom conjunctions. Classical theory (Hajnal–Maass–Pudlák–Szegedy–Turán, FOCS 1987 / JCSS 1993; Siu–Roychowdhury–Kailath, *Discrete Neural Computation*, 1995) says such circuits are cheap — but the attention "gate" is a *linear-fractional* atom, not a clean AND, which is exactly why the circuit intuition does not transfer for free.
- **Attention expressivity (intuition, not a proof tool here)**: the softmax-sharpening / "saturated (hard) attention" limit in which one head implements a soft threshold is folklore — Pérez–Marinković–Barceló (ICLR 2019), Hahn (TACL 2020), Merrill–Sabharwal "Saturated Transformers are Constant-Depth Threshold Circuits" (TACL 2022). These justify the *one-sided* detector but, crucially, the project's own Lemma 13 already characterizes one head **more sharply** than any of these, so they add color, not leverage.

### The most promising rigorous handle
Bypass $\ell_\infty$ bumps entirely and work in the **cleared-denominator invariant** (Lemma 14): $H^{*}(f)\le s$ **iff** there is a strict Boolean-cube sign-representer
$$P(x)=\theta\prod_{r=1}^{s}D_r(x)+\sum_{r=1}^{s}N_r(x)\prod_{g\ne r}D_g(x)$$
with each $(N_r,D_r)$ an admissible affine pair (Lemma 13). This is a degree-$s$ polynomial whose **product structure can encode the within-term AND** and whose **sum structure encodes the OR**, with each $D_r$ sign-uniform but the *product* $\prod_r D_r$ no longer sign-constrained. The target then becomes a concrete algebraic construction problem (choose the $D_r,N_r$ so $P$ strictly sign-represents the union on the cube) rather than an approximation problem blocked by the parallelogram law. This is the route I'd push first.
